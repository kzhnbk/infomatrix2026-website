About Us

About the Project

Advancing cancer treatment through AI-powered genetic variant classification

Our Mission

To accelerate precision medicine by developing machine learning systems that can automatically classify genetic variants, reducing analysis time from several weeks to just a few minutes.

Cancer is one of the most complex diseases, with each patient's tumor harboring unique genetic alterations. Understanding which mutations drive cancer growth and which are benign is crucial for selecting the right treatment.

Our project leverages the power of machine learning and natural language processing to analyze medical literature and clinical notes, providing oncologists with rapid, accurate classification of genetic variants.

The Challenge

Problem & Solution

The Problem

  • Manual variant interpretation takes weeks
  • Expert pathologists are scarce resource
  • Growing backlog of samples to analyze
  • Complex medical literature to review
  • Inconsistent classification standards

Our Solution

  • Instant AI-powered classification
  • Scalable to thousands of samples
  • Consistent, reproducible results
  • NLP for medical text understanding
  • 9-class clinical significance system

Journey

Project Timeline

Data Analysis

Analyzed 3,300+ genetic variant samples with medical literature annotations from available datasets

Feature Engineering

Developed TF-IDF, n-gram, and NER-based features from complex medical texts

Model Development

Trained and compared 6 different ML models including XGBoost and LightGBM

Optimization

Fine-tuned XGBoost achieving 88% accuracy with 0.878 ROC-AUC

Deployment

Launched web application for real-time genetic variant classification
